Facts and Figures

Facts

Impared Water Bodies

According to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), around 46% of assessed rivers and streams, 20% of lakes, and 32% of estuaries in the United States were classified as impaired, meaning they did not meet water quality standards.

Link
Nutrient Pollution

Nutrient pollution remained a significant issue. Excess nutrients, particularly phosphorus and nitrogen, contributed to issues such as harmful algal blooms, hypoxia, and eutrophication in various water bodies.

Link
Clean Water Act Compliance

The EPA reported that, only 21% of assessed rivers and streams fully met water quality standards for key water quality indicators.

Link
Industrial Discharges

The industrial sector continued to be a source of water pollution. Effluents containing chemicals, heavy metals, and other pollutants from industrial activities contributed to the contamination of water bodies.
